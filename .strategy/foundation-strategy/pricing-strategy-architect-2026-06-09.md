## Executive Summary

1:90 Align should price **value-based and deliberately *below* EOS**, using EOS's published economics as the ceiling. EOS clients pay **~£28K–£42K in year one**; that gives enormous room to land a UK SME at a fraction of the cost while still earning a strong founder income. The strategic pricing principle: **anchor against EOS (premium category), undercut on access (UK SME-friendly), and design every tier to pull the customer up the staircase** (services → ongoing → software → certification). No free tier, no race to the bottom against task tools.

---

## Pricing Principles

1. **Value-based, not cost-plus.** You're selling business transformation, not hours. The reference value is "your strategy actually getting executed" — worth far more than the fee to a £5M business.
2. **Anchor high (EOS), price accessible.** EOS at ~£36K/yr makes a £6K–£12K/yr 1:90 engagement look like obvious value — *and* it's still excellent income for a solo founder.
3. **Charge for outcomes/cadence, not tools.** The 90-day cycle is the natural billing unit — it creates built-in recurring revenue.
4. **No free tier (services).** Free attracts non-buyers and burns founder time. A low-risk *paid* entry point (a paid diagnostic) qualifies better.
5. **Price to ascend.** Every offer should make the next one the obvious next step.

## Recommended Pricing Architecture

### Phase 1 — Services (live now, the cash engine)

| Offer | What | Price (recommended) | Type |
|-------|------|---------------------|------|
| **Alignment Diagnostic** | Half-day assessment + 90-day plan outline | **£500–£950** | One-off (qualifier) |
| **90-Day Kickstart** | Full Arc setup + facilitated launch + team onboarding | **£3,500–£6,000** | One-off |

- **Why these numbers**: ~1/4 to 1/6 of EOS's first-year cost — "the operating system EOS-level results without the EOS-level price (or the American franchise)." The Annual Partner at £6K–10K still means **8–12 clients ≈ a healthy solo founder income** and break-even.
- **The Diagnostic is the wedge**: paid (filters tyre-kickers), low-risk, and 70%+ should convert to Kickstart.

### Phase 2 — Certification (the scale engine, Months 9–24)

| Offer | Price (recommended) | Type |

### Phase 3 — Software (deferred, Months 24+)

## Unit Economics at Recommended Pricing

**Services-led (Phase 1), per client:**
- Annual Operating Partner @ £8,000/yr; gross margin ~70% (founder time) → ~£5,600 contribution
- CAC: low (content/referral) — call it £300–£800
- **LTV:CAC easily 8–15:1**; constraint is founder capacity (~12–15 clients max solo)
- **Break-even ≈ 8–10 Annual Partners** → realistic in 6–12 months

**Certification (Phase 2), per coach:**
- £6,000 cert + £1,800/yr licence × ~3yr avg = ~£11,400 LTV per coach, ~90% margin (digital/curriculum)
- 20 coaches = ~£36K cert + ~£36K/yr recurring licence — *and* each coach extends delivery capacity beyond Phil's hours
- **This is where the business breaks the founder-time ceiling.**

## Pricing Tactics

1. **Always show the EOS anchor.** On the pricing/comparison page: "EOS clients pay £36K+/year. 1:90 Align Annual Partner: from £6,000." Frame, don't hide, the comparison.
2. **Price in round, confident £ figures.** £6,000 not £5,997 — this is a premium advisory sale, not e-commerce.
3. **Quarterly billing as default** for recurring — matches the 90-day cycle and improves cash flow.
4. **Don't publish full prices on the site yet (services).** Use "from £X" + a call. High-trust sales convert better in conversation; you also retain pricing flexibility while learning. *Do* publish a clear "from" anchor so you're not invisible.
5. **Raise prices as case studies accumulate.** Early clients get founding-client rates (explicitly framed as such) in exchange for testimonials; prices rise with proof.

---

## What NOT to Do

- ❌ Don't compete on price with Asana/Monday (£8–15/user) — different category, unwinnable, value-destroying.
- ❌ Don't offer a free services tier — burns the one scarce resource (Phil's time).
- ❌ Don't lead with cheap SaaS seats — it caps perceived value of the whole system.
- ❌ Don't discount the method to win the first deals on price — discount on *founding-client status for testimonials* instead, protecting the price anchor.
