1. What Are Cookies

Cookies are small text files that are stored on your device (computer, tablet, or mobile phone) when you visit our website. They contain information about your browsing activity and preferences. Cookies help our website function properly, remember your preferences, and allow us to improve your user experience.

2. How We Use Cookies

We use cookies for several purposes:

  • Essential Cookies: Required for the basic functionality of our website (authentication, security, session management)
  • Analytics Cookies: Help us understand how visitors use our website through Google Analytics
  • Performance Cookies: Collect information about your interaction with our site to optimise performance
  • Functional Cookies: Remember your preferences and settings for a better user experience

4. Third-Party Cookies

We may use third-party services that set their own cookies on your device:

  • Google Analytics: Helps us understand website usage and analyse visitor behaviour. Data is subject to Google's privacy policy.
  • Calendly: Our booking widget uses Calendly for appointment scheduling, subject to Calendly's privacy policy.
  • Stripe: Payment processing is handled by Stripe, which uses cookies for secure payment transactions.

5. Managing Cookies

Most web browsers allow you to control cookies through their settings. You can choose to accept or reject cookies, or be notified when a cookie is being set. Here's how to manage cookies in popular browsers:

  • Google Chrome: Settings > Privacy and Security > Cookies and other site data
  • Mozilla Firefox: Preferences > Privacy & Security > Cookies and Site Data
  • Apple Safari: Preferences > Privacy > Manage Website Data
  • Microsoft Edge: Settings > Privacy, search, and services > Cookies and other site permissions

Please note that disabling cookies may affect the functionality of our website and your ability to access certain features or services.

6. Google Analytics

We use Google Analytics to analyse how visitors interact with our website. Google Analytics uses cookies to collect data about your browsing behaviour, which is used to create reports about website usage. The data collected is anonymous and does not identify you personally.

If you wish to opt out of Google Analytics tracking, you can install the Google Analytics Opt-out Browser Add-on, available at https://tools.google.com/dlpage/gaoptout.
